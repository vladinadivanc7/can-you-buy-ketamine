## What Is Ketamine and Its Uses?
Ketamine is a medication primarily used as an anesthetic in medical settings. Developed in the 1960s, it has gained attention for its rapid-acting antidepressant effects, especially in treating severe depression resistant to other therapies. In 2026, ketamine is approved for specific clinical uses, such as in infusion clinics or as a nasal spray (esketamine) for mental health treatment.

While it has legitimate medical applications, ketamine is classified as a Schedule III controlled substance in many countries, including the US. This means it's available only with a prescription due to its potential for abuse and side effects like dissociation, hallucinations, and dependency.
